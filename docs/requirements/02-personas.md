# CareTaker User Personas

---

## 1. Patient Persona

### Demographics
- **Name**: Margaret Chen
- **Age**: 72
- **Location**: Suburban area, San Francisco Bay Area
- **Family Status**: Widowed, lives alone; daughter lives 45 minutes away
- **Occupation**: Retired school teacher
- **Income**: Fixed pension, moderate

### Background
Margaret has mild mobility issues following a hip replacement 2 years ago. She manages most daily activities independently but needs assistance with:
- Grocery shopping
- Light housework
- Transportation to medical appointments
- Medication reminders

Her daughter, Lisa, helps coordinate her mother's care but cannot be present daily due to work commitments.

### Goals
- Maintain independence and stay in her home
- Have trustworthy, consistent caretakers she can rely on
- Transparent pricing she can afford on a fixed income
- Easy way for her daughter to stay informed about her care
- Feel respected and treated with dignity

### Frustrations
- Previous agency sent different caretakers every visit (no continuity)
- High costs from agency middlemen
- Difficulty communicating with agency when issues arise
- felt like a "task" rather than a person

### Behavioral Patterns
- Prefers phone calls over apps, but comfortable with tablet
- Schedules appointments 1-2 weeks in advance
- Values punctuality highly
- Willing to pay more for consistent, reliable caretakers
- Shares experiences with friends in similar situations

### Technology Comfort
- Basic smartphone usage (calls, texts, photos)
- Uses iPad for video calls with daughter
- Would use a simple, large-button app interface

## 2. Caretaker Persona

### Demographics
- **Name**: David Okonkwo
- **Age**: 34
- **Location**: Oakland, CA
- **Family Status**: Single, one child (daughter, 6 years old)
- **Education**: Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA), pursuing RN degree
- **Work Status**: Currently working through agency, seeking better opportunities

### Background
David has 8 years of experience in elder care and home health. He's certified in:
- CPR/First Aid
- Medication administration
- Mobility assistance
- Dementia care

He currently works for a home care agency but feels frustrated with:
- Agency taking 40% of his earnings
- Limited ability to choose his patients
- No direct relationship with families

### Goals
- Earn more money by cutting out the middleman
- Build his own client base and reputation
- Have flexibility in his schedule (needs evenings free for daughter)
- Grow toward running his own home care business
- Gain positive reviews to increase his visibility

### Frustrations
- Agency fees significantly reduce take-home pay
- No direct feedback from patients (filtered through agency)
- Inflexible scheduling
- No control over which assignments he receives

### Behavioral Patterns
- Always on his smartphone
- Manages his schedule in a paper calendar (transitioning to digital)
- Active on social media, values online reputation
- Networks with other healthcare professionals

### Technology Comfort
- Highly comfortable with smartphones and apps
- Uses WhatsApp for personal communication
- Would benefit from in-app scheduling and payment features
